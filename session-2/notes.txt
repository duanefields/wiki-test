SESSION 2 - "The Sleeper's Name"
Date played: 2026-02-15
Players: 3 (full attendance)

RECAP / WHERE WE LEFT OFF
- Party has cleared the tunnel under the old mill in Dunholt.
- Found a captive woman (unknown identity) in a cage in a ritual chamber.
- Bas-relief of dragon Vaeltharax on the wall. Cultist prisoner tied up.
- Bess Collow still waiting at the Pickled Goat for news.

WHAT HAPPENED

Opening: The chamber
- Party frees the woman from the cage. She's weak, dehydrated, but alert.
- Reveals herself as Lena Croft, a scholar from the Archive of Veth. She was researching the Amber Road disappearances academically and got abducted three nights ago.
- Lena immediately recognizes the bas-relief: "That's not just any dragon. That's Vaeltharax the Unburned — they never found his body after the Battle of the Mirrath Pass. The church just... declared him dead."
- Lena is fascinated by the ritual circle. Vexa stops her from touching it. Tension moment — Lena and Vexa argue about the merits of academic curiosity vs. not dying.

Scene 2: The captive cultist
- Cultist wakes up. His name is Hobb — low-ranking, clearly not a true believer. He joined for food and coin.
- With some roleplay from Mira (persuasion check, nat 20!), Hobb opens up:
  - The cult calls itself "the Ashen Covenant"
  - They answer to someone called "the Hollow Priest" — robed, masked, speaks in a rasping voice
  - Rituals are held at the mill and at a second site — somewhere in the foothills north of Dunholt, near a place called "the Maw"
  - The three missing locals: Tomas the miller's son is alive (kept elsewhere), the merchant was used in a ritual (dead), Petra's fate unknown
  - "They said something big is coming. Something from under the mountain."
- Hobb agrees to cooperate in exchange for safe passage out of town. Aldric is skeptical but allows it.
- Hobb is escorted out of the tunnel by Mira and released at the town's edge. He disappears into the dark.

Scene 3: Morning / returning to the Pickled Goat
- Party returns to Bess. She cries when she hears some of the missing are dead. Pays the 40gp each.
- Lena asks to join the party temporarily to document the cult — Aldric and Vexa say yes, Mira abstains.
- Downtime: Vexa spends the morning on a crafting project — she upgrades Sprocket with a pressure-release reload mechanism. Now gets bonus action reload on a misfire.

Scene 4: Research at the garrison / library
- Aldric sends a letter to his order (the Solrath Vigil) requesting information on Vaeltharax and the Ashen Covenant. Response will take days.
- Lena has notes: the "serpent eating its tail" symbol is called the Ouroboros Shattered — used by cults dedicated to undoing old deaths. Specifically: gods, demigods, and "world-shaping beasts."
- Mira goes to the docks and finds a cult contact — a river trader named Osk who was passing information to the Covenant. She catches him trying to leave town. Intimidation check (success) — Osk reveals the second site is a cave system called the Maw, half a day's ride north. He's let go (reluctantly).

Scene 5: The Road North
- Party sets out north along a hunting trail. Lena comes along, riding a borrowed mule.
- Travel encounter: ambushed by four Covenant scouts. Well-armed, not robed — these are proper guards, not low-ranking recruits.
- Fight in the middle of the road. Notable moments:
  - Vexa's net-bolt misfires (rolled a 2), tangling Aldric briefly — hilarious.
  - Mira kills one scout with a thrown dagger before initiative is rolled (DM ruled it as a surprise round).
  - Aldric uses Divine Smite for the first time this campaign — crits, absolutely destroys one scout.
  - Lena throws a rock. Hits. "I'm not helpless."
- One scout captured. Confirms they were told to watch the road for "investigators."

Scene 6: The Maw
- Party reaches the Maw at dusk — a wide cave mouth in the foothills, reeking of sulfur and ash.
- Signs of recent activity: wheel ruts, discarded torch casings, a fire pit still warm.
- Inside the cave: crude barracks (8 bedrolls, half-eaten food), a storage area with ritual components (sulfur, black salt, jars of blood labeled with names — including "Tomas" and "Petra").
- A locked iron door deeper in. Vexa can hear machinery behind it — rhythmic, like bellows or a pump.
- Mira picks the lock (success on second attempt).

Scene 7: Beyond the door
- A large natural cavern converted into a ritual workspace. Braziers. A stone altar. And a body.
- Petra — dead. Arranged on the altar. Not decayed. Looks peaceful but clearly drained of blood.
- Above the altar, suspended on chains: a massive bronze disc covered in engraved script. Vexa identifies it as a Resonance Engine — a mythological artificer device said to amplify magical energy.
- While examining the disc, the chains begin to creak. Something is activating.
- Then — a voice. From everywhere. Deep, ancient, and amused:
  "You are very small to have come so far. Turn back, and I will consider it mercy."

SESSION ENDED HERE — on the cliffhanger voice.

CLIFFHANGERS
- Whose voice? Is Vaeltharax already communicating through the engine?
- Petra is dead. How do they tell Bess and the town?
- Tomas is still unaccounted for. The blood jars suggest he's being used as a living source.
- The Resonance Engine is activating — what does it do?
- Aldric's letter to the Vigil hasn't been answered yet.

LOOT / XP
- 40 gp each from Bess
- Scout equipment (4 sets, sold for 12gp total = 3gp each)
- Ritual components (sulfur, black salt — Vexa keeps them for crafting)
- Resonance Engine (too large and dangerous to move — noted for later)
- 180 XP each (combat x2, investigation, roleplay, discovery)

DM NOTES
- The voice is Vaeltharax — he's not fully awake but aware. Can communicate through the Engine when it pulses.
- Tomas is alive in a lower chamber — bound, scared, used for blood. Not permanently harmed yet.
- The Hollow Priest has not appeared yet — next session introduction.
- Lena's presence is working well; she's becoming a party favorite NPC.
- Need to address Mira's backstory hook — her brother. Consider a rumor that slavers operate near the mountains.

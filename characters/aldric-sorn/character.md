# Aldric Sorn

**Class:** Paladin of Solrath (sun god)
**Race:** Human
**Level:** 3 (as of Session 3)
**Player:** Marcus

## Voice & Tone

Formal and deliberative. He thinks on the page — he doesn't record events so much as work through
what they mean. He documents moral weight: what was asked of him, what he chose, what he is still
unsure about. He is earnest without being naive, and principled in a way that is increasingly in
tension with what the campaign is showing him about the world.

References to Solrath, the Vigil, and his sense of duty appear throughout. He reflects on others'
honor (and the lack of it). When facing conflict between duty and compassion, he works it out in
writing. He never quite resolves anything neatly.

**Tone:** earnest, principled, sometimes quietly shaken
**Header style:** *"4 Coldmarch · 847"* — precise date, military log format

## Background

Human, age 34. Former soldier, now paladin of Solrath (sun god). Carries a tower shield and
longsword. Deeply principled, terrible at subtlety. He has spent years learning to read men who do
not wish to be read, and it shows. He believes in the proper order of things and is slowly
discovering that the proper order of things may not always be what he was taught.

## Equipment (as of Session 3)

- Tower shield (bearing the blazing eye of Solrath)
- Longsword
- Paladin armor (sun-worn)
- Hollow Priest's partial spellbook — 3 spells: Inflict Wounds, Cause Fear, Necrotic Bolt (homebrew)

## Abilities Used

- **Zone of Truth** (Session 1) — on captured cultist
- **Insight** (Session 1) — read Renfew's fear correctly
- **Divine Smite** (Session 2, first use this campaign) — crit on a scout
- **Lay on Hands** (Session 3) — self-recovery after taking 22 damage and failing WIS save vs. fear

## Relationships

- **Mira Ashveil** — respects her competence. Doesn't push her. Has earned something like trust.
- **Vexa Crankwheel** — genuinely fond of her enthusiasm, occasionally baffled by it.
- **Lena Croft** — approved of her joining the party; found her scholarship unsettling but valuable.
- **Vaeltharax** — deeply troubled. "He did not sound like a monster. That is what frightens me."
- **Captain Renfew** — personally betrayed. Renfew was a soldier. What he did to that identity is
  worse to Aldric than the crimes themselves.
- **The Solrath Vigil** — his order. Has written twice; received no reply. Beginning to wonder why.

## Personal Arc

Sent two letters to the Solrath Vigil requesting information on Vaeltharax and the Ashen Covenant.
No response. The Vigil is not compromised — just slow and bureaucratic — but Aldric doesn't know
that yet. A Vigil knight will eventually arrive, too late to be useful, which will frustrate him.

Increasingly in tension between what he was trained to believe (the Order slew Vaeltharax, end of
story) and what the evidence suggests (the Order entombed a living creature and declared it dead).
This is the central unresolved question of his arc.

# Aldric Sorn

**Class:** Paladin of Solrath (sun god)
**Race:** Human
**Level:** 4 (as of Session 4)
**Player:** Marcus

## Voice & Tone

Formal and deliberative. He thinks on the page — he doesn't record events so much as work through
what they mean. He documents moral weight: what was asked of him, what he chose, what he is still
unsure about. He is earnest without being naive, and principled in a way that is increasingly in
tension with what the campaign is showing him about the world.

References to Solrath, the Vigil, and his sense of duty appear throughout. He reflects on others'
honor (and the lack of it). When facing conflict between duty and compassion, he works it out in
writing. He never quite resolves anything neatly.

**Tone:** earnest, principled, sometimes quietly shaken
**Header style:** *"4 Coldmarch · 847"* — precise date, military log format

## Background

Human, age 34. Former soldier, now paladin of Solrath (sun god). Carries a tower shield and
longsword. Deeply principled, terrible at subtlety. He has spent years learning to read men who do
not wish to be read, and it shows. He believes in the proper order of things and is slowly
discovering that the proper order of things may not always be what he was taught.

## Personal Arc

Sent three letters to the Solrath Vigil. Still no response. The silence is beginning to sit
uncomfortably alongside everything else the campaign is asking him to reconsider.

Increasingly in tension between what he was trained to believe (the Order slew Vaeltharax, end of
story) and what the evidence suggests (the Order entombed a living creature and declared it dead).
This is the central unresolved question of his arc.

The Cradle's ritual inscription — *the blood of the Willing* — is a new theological weight he has
not yet finished carrying. A sacrifice that must consent. This intersects badly with what he knows
about the Covenant's methods and what he suspects about Corvan.
